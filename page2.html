Implementation of Leader Selection Technique for Diabetic Retinopathy Detection - Ongoing

The motivation of the current work is to reduce both the time and space constraints in identification of Diabetic Retinopathy (DR) in an individual. Many of the present existing techniques consume a lot of time mainly for the purpose of training the model. In terms of the evaluation part, most of the pre-trained models can face the problems of overfitting and underfitting. In order to eradicate the following disadvantages, the current work tests the functioning of a Leader Selection Technique (LST) in classification of fundus images depending upon the presence of DR.

Analysis of Uninterruptable Power Supply Critical-to-Quality (CTQ) Parameters - Ongoing

A typical UPS system provides the power backup and electricity to the necessary equipment. Also, inconsistencies, fluctuations and distortions from the power supply are tracked to safeguard the instruments under industrial or domestic usage. Without which, the working of the sensitive hardware like data centers becomes difficult. Abrupt changes in parameters such as voltage, can foment reparations related to both material and economic counterparts. However, UPS systems are still susceptible to failure. The following work involves analysis of the several Critical-To-Quality (CTQ) parameters and their impact on the performance of UPS System. Emphasis is placed on understanding the condition of UPS System using circuitry parameters.
